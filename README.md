# Dynamic JAK-STAT/IFN-signaling in subclinical to clinical T cell-mediated rejection after liver transplantation.

## Study:
Subclinical rejection (SCR) after liver transplantation (LT) precludes immunosuppression withdrawal (ISW) despite normal clinical function. To better understand the immunobiology of early SCR, we performed longitudinal, multimodal immune-profiling and single-cell RNA-sequencing (scRNAseq) pre-transplant to 12-months(M) post-LT in adult living-donor recipients (n=13) participating in an interventional ISW trial. Patients with SCR (termed “nonpermissive”) or quiescent (“permissive”) allografts at 12M exhibited distinct immune trajectories, despite comparable baseline clinical/demographic profiles. Nonpermissive patients showed higher circulating classical monocytes, GM-CSF and CRP pre-transplant, and elevated classical/intermediate monocytes, activated type-1 dendritic cells, and IL-12/IL-17 at 12M. In contrast, permissive patients had greater systemic/intragraft regulatory T-cells, circulating NK cell subsets and fewer PD-1+CD8+ T-cells. scRNAseq revealed early post-reperfusion neutrophil/monocyte activation followed by T-cell-driven inflammation at 12M. Central to these dynamics was biphasic JAK-STAT/Interferon-signaling: greater interferon-stimulated gene (ISG)-expression/JAK-STAT activation occurred post-reperfusion in permissive recipients, which reversed at 12M, when elevated ISG-expression/JAK-STAT signaling was evident in nonpermissive patients (SCR). We then leveraged our findings to ascertain whether a comparable signature existed in internal and external bulk- and scRNA-seq LT cohorts. Across all cohorts, including a rodent model, there was a similar elevation in JAK-STAT signaling with allograft rejection. We also detected distinct, portal-based pSTAT1 staining in a preliminary analysis of biopsies exhibiting histologic rejection compared to non-rejecting controls. Moreover, ruxolitinib-mediated JAK-inhibition suppressed alloreactive CD8+T-cell proliferation and inflammatory-mediator production in-vitro. These exploratory findings suggest a temporal, biphasic role for JAK-STAT signaling in the regulation and occurrence of immune-mediated allo-inflammatory events following LT and implicate JAK-inhibition as a potential therapeutic strategy.

## Data description and availability:
This repository contains all analyses conducted in the manuscript with the corresponding code for transparency and reproducibility. 

Internal datasets: the single-cell RNA-seq (scRNAseq) data from our cohort has been deposited at the Gene Expression Omnibus (GEO), accession number - GSE293039. 

External datasets: Bulk RNA-sequencing data from two external cohorts were obtained and processed as previously described by Londoño et al (PMID: 29709679) and Engel et al. (PMID: 40669521). scRNA-seq data, cell type annotations, and patient information from an external cohort of liver transplant recipients who did or did not develop early allograft dysfunction (EAD) were retrieved from Shao et al. (GSE189539). Similarly, scRNA-seq and paired immune repertoire profiling data were obtained from a recently published cohort of cryopreserved liver biopsies from a predominantly pediatric liver transplant cohort, which included pre-transplant healthy controls, non-TCMR (no histologic rejection, NHR), TCMR, and resolved TCMR biopsies, as reported by Peters et al. (GSE256141), as well as from a rodent model of liver transplant rejection described by Yang et al. (GSE285476).
